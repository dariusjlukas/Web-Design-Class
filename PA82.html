North Island

The North Island has national parks, cities, volcanic activity, and is home to 3/4 of New Zealand's population. It also has the country's largest city: Auckland. The north Island is also the home of New Zealand political activity.

×

South Island

New Zealand's South Island is much less urbanized, and hosts natural landscapes from glaciers to meadows. It is a common vacation spot for those who want to go off the beaten path and explore.

×

Other Islands

From Stewart Island, Chatham, and Subartic Islands, there is plenty to explore in New Zealand's other islands. Much of these islands are national parks, and thus are nearly uninhabited by humans. This allows visitors to experience a landscape untouched for thousands of years.
